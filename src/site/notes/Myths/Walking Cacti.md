__________
Inhabitants of the Yavapai prune the Cholla cacti and other standing groves on account of an old superstition several times a year. Likely out of the simple act of being spooked by a head-height saguaro late at night, though the story goes that some tall cacti are merely asleep during the day and awake at night to snag and steal small children. Bulbous cacti in the early summer might be left next to their beheaded root with smiles carved into them as a pastime.
