*Long in the future on an Earth much like ours, anything standing in defiance of storms will be wiped away. 

*Ancestors with no regard for the future abandoned their planet to its undoing, churning seas that devoured unrecognizable coasts, famines that baked tired soil into clay. The stars above, seen through the bars of a cage- the atmosphere clouded with satellite debris. Most threatening of it all, storms. Unpredictable and vast beyond measure. Certain demise in their winds and lightning, no respite for green plains that have been wiped flat by their passing. Their passing instilled a primal awe- a cataclysm both sudden and impartial, glorious and inescapable.

*The heights of the [[Timeline/Lost Eras\|Lost Eras]] brought the worst storms in human history. In their face, highly specialized clans learned to foresee the oncoming dangers and guide the wayward to safety. The early [[Factions/Stormseekers/Seekers\|Seekers]] learned that in such impenetrable darkness, the only device greater than a perfect deduction was a rare and undoubted intuition.

*Kinder years have followed due to their diligence. Stormseekers have kept travelers and cargo safe on the plains. Their organization demands the highest standards of its practitioners, and a commensurate rate for their trouble. 

*For those fortunate enough to not find themselves in the path of constant peril, cities in the mountains are free of the most sure conditions. Though not everyone has the same perspective on the future, the great darkness of the past has been tempered into a grateful peace. In the relative safety of modernity, historians have traveled their uncharted lands to inspect ruins of bygone times, and learn to walk anew.

*In the bid for mere survival, these lands are simple and beautiful, much like its people. But buried in its earth, floating among its stars, in the depths of its monstrous storms lay the sins of their fathers, and a rot sure to follow wherever their children chart their course.
