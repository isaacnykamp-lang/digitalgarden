*The Dine travel along secret routes with their own Seekers, often refusing to mingle with those who cannot abide by their lifestyle. They’re well-acquainted with the turnings of the [[Locations/Yavapai Desert\|Yavapai Desert]], and can reliably be found in certain places, certain seasons. Their color and history is vibrant, a self-reliant and hardy people who have taken up the Navajo mantle- they insist upon the old ways, despite the state of the world being dire.

*The Nomads have had contact with the god of the Disciples, attributing the Thunderbird to his name. Many signs and great storms follow in his passing. The Great Horned Serpent is this gods foe, and the [[Factions/Basilisk\|Factions/Basilisk]] structures of the [[Seeker’s Guild\|Seeker’s Guild]] often lead the Dine to reject the influence of Guild members, for they may be tainted with evil. The Guild greatly desires the knowledge of Dine Stormwatchers, but their ways cannot be sold.
Semi-familial clans are headed by influential figures and chiefs that meet regularly to direct the path of the nation.

*When Eko was 13, she was tested by a Dine shaman with a trial intended to break her resolve- promised Stormwatching secrets, but secretly misled by the shaman for 4 days of harsh travel East, South, West and North. Though the shaman intended to dissuade her, Eko proved her heart was true and their techniques would be safe with her (annoyed the shaman into relenting with her blithe determination, more like- Eko hardly noticed she was being tested because she didn’t think she would be lied to.) She is, uniquely, highly thought of as a Seeker by the Dine- though the brand of the Thunderbird would not hurt her reputation.

  *On the above, to be struck by lightning is called to the next world- those who have been may receive divine abilities, are purified for new beginnings, and are marked by deities for the spiritual. However, they must not be touched, as the lightning that remains in them may spread illness. They are holy and set apart, but also a danger due to their innate strangeness. Their intentions are not to be trusted, because they no longer think in the same way other humans do.




*Tracker 
*Also called stormwatchers, this micro-faction serves as navigators for the nomadic Dine, specializing in the tell-tale signs of the desert. Nothing evades their eye, and their secrets are held close, especially from the curious hands of the Guild.

*Soothsayer 

*The most esteemed of the watchers, they head parties comprised of their tribes finest. Their attunement with nature and subsequent foresight take precedent over the quarrels of other watchers, but what their elevated senses can be attributed to is a secret known to them. Excellent fighters who are rarely caught by surprise, and well versed in cleansing evils of the spirit.
