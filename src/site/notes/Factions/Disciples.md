*"Life is an ephemeral thing, what is here today is gone tomorrow. Adorn the living with flower and song, and speak for the voiceless."*

*A wandering band of brightly colored devotees, always on the trail of storms and their subsequent patron god, [[Gods/The Host\|The Host]]. 
Disciples are clad in well-worn fabrics of intense colors, typically blue and red, and wear the [[Fool Sigil\|Fool Sigil]] somewhere on their person. Intensely non-nuclear, their lifestyle discourages material attachment and ownership, preferring to share according to the greatest need. Everyone works, everyone eats. They're surprisingly pluralistic, allowing other religious figures to coexist with theirs- so long as generosity and faith are rewarded. Their golden rule is 'The Two: A flower for you, a song for me.' In essence, all gifts precipitate a gift in following, and rarely in the same form. 
The point of contention is between those who have seen and not seen [[Gods/The Host\|The Host]]. The Disciples will point to lightning sprites as a sign of his presence in the storms of his wake, and many have joined the Disciples because they claim to have seen a deity when surviving a wreck. A select few, often Seekers, believe the Host to be calamitous at best, a fell god at worst. Whatever is true, the Disciples say he 'speaks with no voice,' ergo their eager proselytising is required. Most of their group will claim to have seen him some way or another, but those who have not, of which there are many, are exhorted to believe harder. While those who are not witnesses are zealous, it's not always out of the best intent; trying to prove their faith with works and see their god. 

*Their reputation with others is that they're insane. But not unwelcome. If you can stomach their incessant preaching, they're generous in all things except one. 
Their fabrics are to die for, but in this one field, they demand proper payment. The shadows of rainfalls are where the ephemeral [[Creatures/Petrichor Silkmoths\|Petrichor Silkmoths]] are collected and harvested. The material is peerless and provides the monetary backbone for the group’s philanthropy.
*Uniquely versed in the practice of grinding any interesting oddment they find into a fine powder- their vats of dye are essential ingredients to fabric makers and need lots of hauling. The additional dye can be carted into cities for some extra cash.
The professional weavers and tailors tend to be more lax on the rules of faith, and follow the religious sect casually as a mantra. After all, they're busy doing the hard work. Once again, this separates the pure of heart and the 'witnesses' from the lukewarm; the religious leaders argue often about dependence on their merchants and how it tarnishes their life mission for both parties.

*Their tailors are ever busy, either at their looms or tending to their charge of silkworms. Something between traveling entomologist and weaver, their passion for the craft is without equal. Trails of colorful fabric mark their passing, and all who haggled for their durable wares. Tight enough thread is waterproof! 
*How they spin is no secret. Nobody else can match them.

*This isn't to say that all of the Disciples are so well meaning...
*some use their troupe’s warmth and safety to make thralls of destitute converts, inducting them into a cult of ideation, exploiting god and order. Survivors of wrecks may meet these Disciples and gratefully accept their aid, only to find strings attached that would see their labor and emotions taken advantage of, and their faith proven in service with no end.
