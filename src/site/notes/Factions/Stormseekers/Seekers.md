*The titular occupation, a group of wandering specialists that provide the front line of defense against the omnipresent rains. A loosely connected sequence of hubs are present in most affluent cities to provide structure for the domineering Guild. Potential clients meet at the [[Loza forum\|Loza forum]] hub to discuss contracts and rates with available Seekers with no interference from the organizers. For their services and available records and equipment, all Seekers pay a cut of their earnings to fund the guild.

*The education of future Seekers is largely informal. There are a couple of schools which provide a sparse application of meteorology, but students rarely prosper as field training is paramount to success. Typically, a Seeker will parse out some teaching to a young follower during the pastime of their deployment, and the child will learn as much as they can from any others that follow. A promising pupil might be formally apprenticed if the situation allows, being protected by the informed calls of their teacher and gaining invaluable experience. At a time of the teacher or students discretion, they will take the [[Band Test\|Band Test]], a 4 day trial proctored by the Guild. 
The general exception is [[Locations/Pharus\|Pharus]]'s [[Factions/Stormseekers/Organization/Deucalion Arcanum\|Deucalion Arcanum]], premiere schooling for [[Factions/Stormseekers/Seekers\|Seekers]] suited to academics.

*The Guild treats any measure of bribery as insubordination and applicants who intend to will be expelled, with no chance of grace. Their infallible reputation must be without blemish.

*Students that pass the test receive a [[Graduation Band\|Graduation Band]] and gain access to the premier rates that the station offers. As an official Seeker, they receive numerous benefits and a potential lifetime of well-rewarded work.

*The test can be re-taken. But repeated failures may see a Seeker forced to ply their trade without certification. Working for undesirable rates in dangerous towns, anyone who is desperate enough to hire a Seeker without certification is likely not the best client. Often, this means working for commercial fishing in towns that don’t take kindly to the austere presentation, a life of non-identity.

*This is not universal, some get the education just to better serve their caravan to a quiet life.

*The Guild usually asks their Seekers to report logs to maintain records and request better at-demand services, their primary function. As record-keepers, Seekers can earn a number of extraneous certifications and awards from the Guild based on their prolificacy. The [[Honor Band\|Honor Band]], which is relevant in the narrative, is one of the most revered awards that can be proffered, awarded to next of kin or reporter for a Seeker sacrificing their life ensuring the survival of their host. Most prefer honourifics that don’t require you to die, naturally.

*Record-keeping means clients can request a particular specialist if they would prefer repeat services. The Guild also maintains obituaries for the inevitable, and works closely with charting institutes to keep their tracers best equipped with the latest data.

*The average Seeker is one of an odd bunch. It takes a bit of a unique individual to spend one’s life on the run from oncoming weather. As a rule, they’re enigmatic when not skittish, prudish when not chaste, and collectors of oddments with incredible perceptive abilities. Most of them, at least. They’re heavy under the thumb of their administration, so some take their field work as time to cut loose. Never debauched, the one thing they have in common is a pride in their work, earned from decades of grueling study.

Foreseeing the coming need for an antiquated means of navigation, they are responsible for the durable [[waystones\|waystones]] that pockmark the winding paths of the valleys below. Their archive halls are littered with woodcut and bas-reliefs of primordial Seekers, and the original [[Myths/Storm Shepherd\|Storm Shepherd]].

Some worship the [[Gods/Roko\|Roko]] as a forlorn god, unknown to them is the origin of the symbol.
