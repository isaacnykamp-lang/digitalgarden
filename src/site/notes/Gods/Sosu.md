>*"In the time before all things, there was absence. Presence would come to know itself with time, but absence, like presence, merely is. It will remain forever, and while it is there, nothing remains. 
>When [[Gods/Mother Abyss\|Mother Abyss]] howls, is it not of sorrow? The first creations of the lightless [[Gods/Moon Goddess\|Moon Goddess]] were tears.  Such stillness cannot remain, even our air is filled with their breath- emptiness is the grave our bodies, but not souls, are subjected to. The silence endured, and the primordial beings waited.
>Fullness was the first gift of [[Gods/Sosu\|Sosu]], wind and motion that followed her outpouring of radiance. Presence that knew itself. The moon rejoiced in this gift, the Abyss below rejected it, for it only made its lonesome hunger that much stronger. More to have meant there was more it did not have already, and her flame was love.
>Now the moon guides those who venture closest to the dark, so that even the lost know [[Gods/Sosu\|Sosu]]'s light would come with time. There is no corner or shadow to be left unlit.
>The Sun and Moon loved each other dearly for so long, time cannot speak to it. Despite their radiant dance, stillness sought to interrupt their orbits. The Moon feared that their time together was bound to end, that the emptiness would return as it always does. So many tremors had come and gone, flames that sputtered to life only to wilt; the Sun had never so much as known sorrow, and did not see how it could their dance could ever end. Her vibrance was enduring and strong... her temper equally brash. A zeal for life that could not be sated, and an anger not so easily redirected- she could conjure endless shapes of life, only to burn them away in frustration that her vision was not met. The only design she truly treasured was a mould of life the Moon had devised from their dance.
>They sought to dance yet, but the stillness between them increased. As the Moon retreated from a tempestuous flame, her resigning heart left a space for her old foe to speak into. While ever she hoped for Sosu to temper herself, she had little faith in it. After all, gods are not so good at changing.
>As they built their paradise together, the Sun soured that so much of the Earth would be shadowed from her light. Should not their creations be protected from the edges of the Abyss?
>Finally the Moon, so far away now and her hands scarred from holding her flame, spoke in rage. How foolish was this young Sun that she could not see what her hands could scorch even before her?  All their creations the had scorched before? Prideful and afraid, they fought until their Earth shattered into mountains and ravines, the maw of the Abyss opened under their feet. The Moon fled, knowing this end was inevitable and seeking to do not more harm.
>The last creation of the Sun were tears. 
>When the Sun looked into the Abyss and saw hunger, she truly knew silence for the first time- and saw herself in its eternal loneliness. Fearing they would never dance again, something she was once so certain in, she desperately bid to avoid this loneliness ere her flame would fade, and decided in finality that even [[Gods/Mother Abyss\|Mother Abyss]] could become Fullness.
>Her tears in the ocean of abyss created [[World Elements/Dusk\|Dusk]]. The Sun's warmth and the dark of the Abyss formed the soul, and the body they had moulded together from their dance, almost a perfect replacement. Which is why we say Sosu's last creation were tears; even she knew it to be a mistake to steal it. But she could not bear to be alone.
  The humans she enlivened would not last, while they had her spirit, their flesh was so weak she could not touch them lest they scald. The darkness in their heart inherited the emptiness even she feared. The evil they could fall to in the silence appalled her. 
>But their faces were hers, and she would waste no tear, the last reminder of the Moon she had. It had been a sin, and hers alone to make humans for her own selfish ends. 
>But she had known sorrow now, and would not leave them behind. She would know sorrow for eons to come.
>
>In time the moon returned, fearing for what might have occurred in bitterness. But the humans they devised together bore her face alone, and their souls had tears in them. 
>When she understood what had happened, she mourned that an innocent soul would be driven to sorrow only by her tainted fears. That their creation would forever bear the stain of their brokenness, ever drifting into the darkness.
>She found Sosu weary and dimming on the shoals of the sea. The Sun did not move to meet her, but told her with an empty voice what had been transpiring.
>The young humans shrank away from her light, drawn to the abyss by the hollow of their souls the moment she departed, fearing she would never return. While she trawled the ocean to retrieve their light every night, the call of [[Gods/Mother Abyss\|Mother Abyss]] was ever tempting their hearts to despair. After enough toil, her flame dimmed until she could no longer muster her strength to leave the shoals. On the [[First Black Day\|First Black Day]], there was no sun. Though she could never stop loving, her sin was too great for creation, for her to bear. Perhaps they should never have danced at all.
>
>And the Moon told her that she would never have seen it undone. Even if she knew of this end, she wouldn't take back one dance.
>Though her fear had come to fruition before her eyes, the Moon had lived long enough to know.
>All the emptiness in eternity cannot put out a single flame on it's own.
>
>[[Gods/The Host\|The Host]] is the product of their first covenant. The product of burnt hands and a declaration of eternal faith; to each other, and their creation, that those whole hold their Sigils close would forever be sheltered from the Abyss and its draw, and those who offered their faith through sacrifice would earn their devotion.
>One to see the future without eyes,
>One to to heal the present without hands,
>One to speak of the past with no voice.
>And with faith, and the temperance befitting of gods, they embraced without burning, and mended their creation to their greatest extent. 
>But fell forces had grown in the consort of the malicious dark, presence that knows absence, Abyss that seeks to claim its own ever hungry.
>The Moon must stay on her course in the dark, rarely to meet the eye of her beloved and ever secretive in her movements. Their celestial bodies are a reminder of eternal devotion; sunless days, moonless nights are a reminder that this covenant requires most the completion of their creation. For now, we know that love blossoms into tears, and tears into joy; and an eclipse sees them dance again. Which is the story my father told me verbatim when I was foolish enough to ask of the [[Eclipse Festival\|Eclipse Festival]]. So don't ask again."

-*[[Anselmo Solis\|Anselmo Solis]], Last Elder of the South Villages
